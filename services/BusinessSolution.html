Enterprise Resource Planning

  • Precision is future of Analytics
  • Organizations will have to create more precise systems for measurement, classification and decision support
  • Business analytics (BA) is a long-term program, not a project.
  • 1dentify the business objectives that are key to delivering a corporate strategy, and understand the metrics that will be used to measure success.
  • Define the appropriate sponsor and organizational structure, such as the business intelligence competency center (BICC), to support the business, analyst/IT requirements, priorities and efforts.
  • Define (at a high level) how business intelligence tools, technologies and applications can help measure, monitor and manage the achievement of business objectives.
  • evaluate existing information infrastructures and prepare to incorporate additional federated data models to support the expansion of the volumes, velocity, variety and validity of new information.
  • Complement existing investments in BI with data discovery and visualization, predictive analytics, modeling and simulation and massive storage and processing capabilities.
  • Get business users to take a leadership role in the BI initiative — without their full engagement your investment in BI will never realize its full potential.
  • Data quality problems are inevitable, so connect your data quality and BI programs from the outset.
